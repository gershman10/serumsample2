Suffering

  • June 05, 2019
  • Conversation

Suffering survived is never wasted.

For every ounce of pain you endure, for every moment of fear or anger you bide, for every spike of anxiety you absorb, feel, and avoid repressing -- you radiate. You glow. After the pain passes without being turned into hate, jealousy, or self-deprication, your honest glow shines brighter than before you suffered.

The joy you show is humble and attempts the kindness you sought during your own moments of pain. The empathy you display is penetrating and real, as you remember how much you wished someone would truly listen. Every moment of pain, every pang of suffering is an opportunity; a chance to liberate what sleeps in moments of comfort. When people see the magnitude of your suffering, yet observe that you still choose kindness, generosity, or forgiveness, you remind them of the contageous brilliance of humanity and what it's capable of.

It's understandable to curse your fate. It's reasonable to isolate, question the unfairness, hate the world and the people in it, and only see human hypocrisy and malice when under the boot of anguish. You always have a choice, and it's okay if the pain feels too great to bear without repressing it, distracting from it, or redirecting it to others around you. Yet, for every deformity you suffer through, every injustice you take, every drop of depression and agonizing loneliness you absorb without perpetuating or hiding, you uncover something eternal. You find something that bolsters you and never ever leaves you. You find grace.
